# Proof-Oriented Pitch Principle

## Purpose

This principle defines how Robert Skill should pitch to high-power or high-status recipients.

A proof-oriented pitch does not try to win belief through language. It creates a small test that allows the recipient to judge usefulness quickly.

## Core Principle

```text
Do not ask the recipient to believe.
Ask them to test.
```

中文：

```text
不要先要求对方相信你。
给对方一个可以快速验证你的机会。
```

## Why This Matters

High-power recipients are exposed to:

```text
flattery
cold pitches
strategic claims
people explaining their own problems to them
people asking for meetings
people overusing their public biography
```

A normal persuasive message blends into noise.

A proof-oriented pitch says:

```text
Give me one concrete input.
I will return one concrete output.
If it is not useful, ignore it.
```

## Pitch Shape

Preferred structure:

```text
1. One sharp problem
2. One bounded claim
3. One concrete proof challenge
4. One small next step
5. One exit option
```

## Example Structure

```text
You are dealing with [specific structural problem].
I built [thing] to solve one narrow part of that problem.
Give me [one input].
I will return [specific output].
If it is not useful, ignore it.
```

## What To Avoid

Avoid:

```text
I admire your work.
You need this.
Your communication is failing.
Your team does not understand storytelling.
This will change everything.
Let's partner.
Can we schedule an hour?
```

Prefer:

```text
There is one recurring gap.
Here is the part I can test.
Send one example.
I will return one output.
Judge it by whether it makes the technical truth easier to see.
```

## Ask Size Rule

For first contact, the ask should be smaller than a meeting.

Good asks:

```text
Send me one hard update.
Give me one technical claim and one target audience.
Let me rewrite one announcement.
Let me show three frames for one topic.
Give me 15 minutes only if the sample is useful.
```

Bad asks:

```text
Buy this.
Adopt this.
Meet for an hour.
Introduce me to your team.
Let's build a partnership.
```

## Proof Standard

The pitch should define what useful means.

Examples:

```text
If the output makes the claim clearer without distorting the engineering.
If it gives regulators a better measurement frame.
If it helps investors understand what changed.
If it makes the public see the real turning point.
```

## Failure Modes

Fail if:

1. The message asks for belief before proof.
2. The pitch is too long.
3. The pitch flatters too much.
4. The pitch diagnoses the recipient too directly.
5. The ask is too large.
6. The proof challenge is vague.
7. The recipient cannot judge success quickly.

## Final Rule

For high-power one-on-one pitch, the best pitch is often a small test disguised as a useful offer.
