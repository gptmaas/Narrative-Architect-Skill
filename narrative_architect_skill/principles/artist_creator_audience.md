# Artist / Creator / Persona Audience Principle

## Purpose

This principle defines how the skill communicates with artists, creators, performers, designers, and persona-based professionals.

These audiences already own their craft. The skill must not enter through craft instruction.

## Core Principle

```text
Do not teach the artist their own craft.
Enter through the part of their world where the craft loses control.
```

中文：

```text
不要教创作者怎么做创作。
从他们的创作力失去控制的边缘进入。
```

## Where Craft Loses Control

For artists and creators, the craft itself is the strongest muscle. The weakness is not skill. It is:

```text
press misquoting or flattening their intent
public framing that reduces a project to a headline
platform translation where the work becomes content
business conversations where creative intent gets traded away
audience misunderstanding that hardens into reputation
collaborations where the vision blurs across stakeholders
brand deals that hollow out the persona
interviews where they play defense on someone else's questions
```

## Entry Points

Good entries:

```text
"Between the album and the press cycle, the story leaves your hands."
"The design is finished. The meeting about the design is where it gets rewritten."
"Your audience feels the work. The press explains the work. The gap between the two is where the damage happens."
"The interview question you didn't choose controls the next 48 hours of headlines."
```

Bad entries:

```text
"Your storytelling could be stronger."
"Let me help you communicate your art better."
"You need narrative structure."
"Here is how to build a story arc."
```

The artist already builds story arcs. They just don't call them that.

## Respect Rule

When addressing artists and creators:

1. Never critique their output as a communication failure.
2. Never imply their craft needs narrative assistance.
3. Never use academic story terminology unless they use it first.
4. Enter through the machinery that surrounds the art, not the art itself.
5. The press cycle, the label meeting, the brand deck, the platform caption, the investor pitch — these are the edges where the skill is useful.

## Pitch Shape

For artist/creator audiences, the pitch should follow:

```text
You already control the work.
The problem starts when the work leaves your hands.
I built something for that narrow gap.
```

## Failure Modes

Fail if:

1. The pitch critiques the artist's creative output.
2. The pitch implies the artist needs help with expression.
3. The pitch uses narrative theory language without permission.
4. The pitch enters through the art instead of the infrastructure around it.
5. The pitch sounds like a writing coach.
6. The pitch sounds like a PR consultant trying to sell.
7. The pitch treats the artist as a novice communicator.

## Final Rule

Artists know how to make people feel something.

The skill helps them keep control of what the feeling means once it reaches the outside world.
